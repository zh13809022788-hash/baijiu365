# Content Standard

## Core Rule

Every page must help a real reader make a better decision about baijiu.

## Required Qualities

A strong page should include:

- A clear answer in the first screen
- Practical context, not only history
- A point of view
- Specific examples
- Tradeoffs and risks
- Comparisons to familiar spirits when useful
- A short FAQ for search intent coverage
- Internal links to related guides

## Avoid

Do not write pages that sound like:

- Generic AI travel/food content
- Brand propaganda
-招商软文
- Over-polished cultural slogans
- Vague phrases such as “rich aroma,” “long history,” and “smooth finish” without explanation
- Fake authority claims

## Preferred Voice

Clear, practical, independent, and curious.

The site can respect baijiu culture without sounding ceremonial. It should explain baijiu to outsiders without flattening it into vodka, sake, or whisky.

## Page Formula

For most guides:

1. Direct answer
2. Why it matters
3. Practical explanation
4. Examples or comparison table
5. Mistakes to avoid
6. Buying/drinking/context notes
7. FAQ
8. Related reading

## Trust Standard

If a claim depends on current prices, product availability, legal import rules, or brand-specific facts, mark it as needing verification unless there is a reliable source.

If future pages include affiliate or supplier relationships, disclose them clearly.
